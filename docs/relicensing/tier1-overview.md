# RFC86 Tier 1 Institutional Consent — Quick Overview

This page is a short, public summary for institutional/team leads reviewing Tier 1 consent for cBioPortal relicensing.

## What is being proposed

cBioPortal is proposing to relicense in-scope project repositories from **AGPL-3.0** to **Apache-2.0** under RFC86.

In-scope repositories:

- `cBioPortal/cbioportal` (backend)
- `cBioPortal/cbioportal-frontend`
- `cBioPortal/cbioportal-docker-compose`
- `cBioPortal/cbioportal-core`
- `cBioPortal/session-service`

## Why this change

- Required by key funders that mandate permissive licensing.
- Removes adoption friction associated with AGPL in industry settings.
- Apache-2.0 provides explicit patent terms expected by many legal teams.

## What Tier 1 asks from institutional leads

Tier 1 is institutional/team-lead consent for contributions made under institutional sponsorship/employment, for the repositories above.

The project now uses one consolidated document instead of separate letters:

- [`outreach-tier1-letter.md`](outreach-tier1-letter.md) (Institutional Consent Sheet)

## What Tier 1 does not replace

Tier 1 does not replace Tier 2 individual outreach where contributions may be outside institutional employment/sponsorship.
